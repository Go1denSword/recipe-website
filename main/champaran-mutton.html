Ahuna (Champaran) Mutton

Idk how to make this i just like to eat this so recipie from ai


Prep Time: 2 Hrs 30 Mins Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Mutton (curry cut)
  • 750g Onions (finely sliced)
  • 150ml Mustard Oil
  • 3-4 Whole Garlic Bulbs
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 3-4 Dry Red Chilies
  • 2 Bay Leaves (Tejpatta)
  • 1 tbsp Turmeric Powder
  • 2 tbsp Coriander Powder
  • 1 tbsp Cumin Powder
  • 1 tbsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder
  • Whole Spices (Cloves, Cardamom, Cinnamon)
  • Salt to taste
  • Wheat dough (for sealing the pot)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the Marinade In a large mixing bowl, combine the cleaned mutton, sliced onions, ginger-garlic paste, ground spices (turmeric, coriander, cumin, red chili), and salt.
  2. Heat the Mustard Oil Heat the mustard oil in a pan until it reaches the smoking point (to remove the pungent raw smell). Let it cool down slightly, then pour half of it into the meat marinade. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 45 minutes.
  3. Prepare the Earthen Pot (Handi) Pour the remaining cooked mustard oil into the earthen pot, swirling it to coat the bottom and sides. Add the whole spices, dry red chilies, bay leaves, and whole garlic bulbs.
  4. Assemble Transfer the marinated mutton and onions into the handi. Do not add any extra water, as the onions and meat will release their own juices.
  5. Seal the Handi Place the lid on the handi. Take the wheat dough and wrap it around the edge of the lid to completely seal it, trapping all the steam inside (Dum process).
  6. Slow Cook Place the handi on a very low flame (traditionally charcoal embers). Cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours. Crucial step: Every 15-20 minutes, grab the handi using a thick cloth and gently shake/swirl it to prevent the meat from sticking to the bottom. Do not open the seal.
  7. Serve Once cooked, break the dough seal carefully to release the fragrant steam. Check that the mutton is tender and falls off the bone. Serve hot with steamed rice, litti, or roti.
